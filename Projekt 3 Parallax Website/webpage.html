Cedomir Mitic

Various musical options are available for this purpose. Either thematic passages of the musical work are presented in an abbreviated form or motifs that do not belong to the topic at all are chosen. The intro often reveals something typical about the song. [1] It is mostly instrumental, but there are also vocal variants. Very often succinct riffs are used by guitars (e.g. in the song Somethin' Stupid ) or keyboards ( Whole Lotta Love by Led Zeppelin , May 1969; Smoke on the Water by Deep Purple , December 1971). A violin intro presents Roll Over Beethovenby the Electric Light Orchestra (September 1972); a long piano intro in Fats Domino's The Fat Man reveals clear boogie-woogie rhythms (December 1949), and drums introduce the popular We Will Rock You in Queen (September 1977). A spoken intro is heard on Scatman (Ski-Ba-Bop-Ba-Dop-Bop) by Scatman John (January 1995), a 48 second a cappella intro precedes the Queen song Bohemian Rhapsody (August 1975). .
